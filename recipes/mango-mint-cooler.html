Mango Mint Cooler

This Mango Mint Cooler is the ultimate refreshing drink for hot summer days. Made with ripe mango, fresh mint, a splash of lime, and chilled soda water, it is fruity, fizzy, and incredibly satisfying. It comes together in minutes and requires no special equipment. Both kids and adults love it — and it is easy to make a big batch for parties or gatherings.

Serving Options for Mango Mint Cooler

This cooler can be served in a variety of ways depending on your preference. It is equally delicious as a still drink, a fizzy spritzer, or even blended into a smoothie-style version. Adjust the sweetness and ice level to your liking.

  • Fizzy Version: Top each glass with chilled soda water or sparkling water just before serving. Stir gently to preserve the bubbles.
  • Still Version: Skip the soda water and top with regular chilled water or coconut water for a lighter, non-fizzy alternative.
  • Blended Smoothie Style: Blend all ingredients including ice cubes until smooth. Pour directly into glasses and serve immediately with a straw.

What to Serve With Mango Mint Cooler

This cooler pairs wonderfully with light snacks and summer foods. Serve it alongside grilled chicken skewers, fish tacos, or a fresh fruit chaat for a complete summer spread. It also complements spicy dishes beautifully — the sweetness of the mango and the coolness of mint help balance out the heat.

For a fun presentation at parties, serve in mason jars with colorful paper straws, a mint sprig garnish, and a slice of mango on the rim.

Ingredients

  • 2 large ripe mangoes, peeled and diced
  • ¼ cup fresh mint leaves, plus extra for garnish
  • 3 tablespoons fresh lime juice
  • 2 tablespoons honey or sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 2 cups chilled water
  • 1 cup soda water or sparkling water
  • 2 cups ice cubes
  • ¼ teaspoon black salt or regular salt

Directions

Step 1: Add diced mango, mint leaves, lime juice, honey, and 2 cups of chilled water to a blender. Blend until completely smooth.

Step 2: Pour the mixture through a fine mesh strainer into a large pitcher, pressing with a spoon to extract all the juice. Discard the pulp or reserve for another use.

Step 3: Stir in black salt. Taste and adjust sweetness or lime juice as needed. Refrigerate for at least 20 minutes until well chilled.

Step 4: Fill glasses with ice cubes and pour the chilled mango mixture about three-quarters of the way up. Top each glass with soda water and stir gently.

Step 5: Garnish with a sprig of fresh mint and a mango slice. Serve immediately and enjoy!
